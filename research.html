Ontario Native Plants

Black-eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta)

Type: Wildflower

Bloom Time: Summer

Description: A hardy perennial with bright yellow petals and a dark center, great for pollinators.

Canada Anemone (Anemone canadensis)

Type: Ground cover

Bloom Time: Spring to early summer

Description: Spreads easily and produces white, buttercup-like flowers. Thrives in moist soil.

Eastern Red Columbine (Aquilegia canadensis)

Type: Perennial wildflower

Bloom Time: Spring

Description: Nodding red and yellow flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies.

Ontario Native Animals

Eastern Chipmunk (Tamias striatus)

Type: Mammal

Habitat: Woodlands, gardens

Description: Small, striped rodents that store food in cheek pouches. Common in forested areas and backyards.

Great Blue Heron (Ardea herodias)

Type: Bird

Habitat: Wetlands, rivers, lakes

Description: Tall, graceful bird with a long neck and legs. Feeds on fish and amphibians.

Painted Turtle (Chrysemys picta)

Type: Reptile

Habitat: Ponds, marshes, slow-moving water

Description: Colorful turtle with red and yellow markings. Often seen basking on logs.
